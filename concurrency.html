Concurrency

Business technology made simple.

Read time 4 minutes

My Role

I was the UX lead working to define the experience strategy, perform a content audit, create and present deliverables to stakeholders, design and test the new information architecture, wireframing, prototyping, style guide creation, and usability testing the final responsive design.

The Team: Emery Ullenberg, Jon Tetzlaff, Keith Donnell

The Challenge

Create a responsive site that cleverly explains an highly-complex suite of products and technologies in a way that's both helpful for those not-in-the-know, and useful for expert technologists.

The Research

It's a rare thing for a design studio to work with a client that shares so much in common with them. Getting to know Concurrency and their users was a treat, because it meant getting to know ourselves better at the same time.

Workshopping

Our initial discovery kickoff with Concurrency had us running goal exploration, persona creation, and stakeholder alignment exercises to construct the initial hypothesis that would drive our research.

Content Audit

Before the process of restructuring any site begins, we perform a Content Audit on the current site, app, or system. This allows the team to find large content holes, uncomfortable flows, and grasp the scope of the project.

Information Architecture / Sitemap

After mapping the system, running tree tests and card sorts with team members, stakeholders, and current users, we'll reorganize the structure and visualize the flow. This low-cost deliverable allows everyone involved to instantly grasp the scope of the project and guides discussions with the engineers and designers.
